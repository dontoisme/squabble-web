Squabble lets you speed up or slow down narration without losing your place, and jump forward or back by a fixed number of seconds with the skip buttons. This page covers changing speed mid-book, and where the skip amount gets set.

## Changing playback speed

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the speed button">
    It's at the bottom of the player, showing your current multiplier like <strong>1x</strong>.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a preset speed">
    Choose anywhere from 0.5x (half speed) up to 3x: 0.5x, 0.75x, 1x, 1.25x, 1.5x, 1.75x, 2x, 2.5x, or 3x. It applies instantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Screenshot src="/help/player/speed-sheet.png" alt="Playback Speed sheet showing preset speeds from 0.5x to 1.75x with 1x currently selected" caption="Pick a speed — it applies immediately." />

<Callout variant="tip">
A slightly faster speed, like 1.25x, saves real time on a long book without making narration feel rushed. Slow down to 0.75x or 0.5x for dense passages, or when you're drifting off to sleep.
</Callout>

## Skip forward and back

The circular buttons on either side of Play show the number of seconds they'll jump — 15 seconds back and 30 seconds forward by default. Tap either one any time to skip without breaking stride.

These intervals aren't adjustable from inside the player itself. To change how far each button jumps, or to set the speed every new book should start at, head to [Playback Settings](/help/settings/playback-settings).

<Callout variant="info">
Speed and skip intervals are two different kinds of setting: a speed change you make in the player only lasts for that session, while your defaults in Settings apply to every book you open from then on.
</Callout>

The same skip buttons — and whatever speed you last picked — carry over to your lock screen and CarPlay controls. See [Background, Lock Screen & CarPlay](/help/player/background-carplay-lockscreen).
