Bookmarks let you flag a specific moment in a book so you can jump straight back to it later, with an optional note to remind you why it mattered.

## Adding a bookmark

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Bookmarks">
    Tap the bookmark icon in the player's bottom toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the + button">
    This opens a field for an optional note.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a note and confirm">
    Type up to 200 characters, then tap <strong>Add Bookmark</strong>. Or leave it blank and tap <strong>Add Bookmark</strong> to save the moment with no note attached.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout variant="tip">
Long-press the bookmark icon instead of tapping it to drop a bookmark at your current position instantly, without opening the sheet first.
</Callout>

## Viewing and managing bookmarks

The **Bookmarks** sheet lists everything you've added, each showing its timestamp and chapter. Tap any bookmark to jump straight to it. Your own bookmarks can be edited or removed — tap the pencil icon to change the note, or the trash icon to delete it after confirming.

## Furthest Position

Alongside anything you add yourself, Squabble automatically keeps one more marker: **Furthest Position** — the furthest point you've reached in the book. It updates as you listen and can't be edited or deleted; it's always there, always current.

<Callout variant="spoiler">
Furthest Position isn't just a convenience — it's the anchor Squabble uses to decide what's spoiler-safe. A [Timestamp Comment](/help/player/timestamp-comments) unlocks for you exactly when your Furthest Position passes it, whether you're actively listening there or you rewind afterward to replay a scene.
</Callout>

If you lose your place — say a Sleep Timer stopped playback overnight — open Bookmarks and tap **Furthest Position** to snap straight back to it.
