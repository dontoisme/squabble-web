Once your [Audiobookshelf](/help/audiobookshelf/what-is-audiobookshelf) server is connected, its whole library is browsable from inside Squabble. This page covers finding a book, downloading it, and what happens if a download goes missing. <PlatformBadge platform="mobile" />

## Browsing your library

Tap **Browse Library** to see your server's books as cards with cover art, title, author, and length. A few things make it easier to find what you want:

- Use the search bar to filter by title or author as you type.
- If your server has more than one library, chips at the top let you switch between them.
- Books that belong to a series can surface as a **Series** result, grouping the whole set together.

Tap any book to open its details — description, chapters, narrator, genres, and identifiers like ASIN or ISBN, when your server has them.

## Downloading a book

Downloading pulls the audio file from your server onto your device and adds the book to your library, same as any local import.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a book's details">
    From the library browser, tap the book you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Import to Library">
    Squabble downloads the file and shows progress as a percentage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to your library">
    When the download finishes, choose <strong>Go to Library</strong> to jump straight to it, or stay where you are.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout variant="info">
A book already in your library shows <strong>Already in Library</strong> instead of a download button, so you won't end up with duplicates.
</Callout>

## Keeping your place in sync

While you listen to a book that came from Audiobookshelf, Squabble periodically reports your position back to the server. That keeps your progress lined up with anything else that reads from the same server — you're not stuck picking up where you left off in only one app.

This is separate from the Ghost Markers and Furthest Position your guildmates see — those keep working the same way they do for any book in your [library](/help/library/importing-books).

## Reconnecting when a download goes missing

If a downloaded file goes missing from your device — after a reinstall, for example — its library card offers to re-download instead of import from scratch.

- If your server is still connected, Squabble asks to confirm, then re-downloads the file. Your progress and chapters are already preserved, so you won't lose your place.
- If you're no longer connected, Squabble asks you to reconnect first. Once you're signed back in, the re-download picks up from there.

<Callout variant="warning">
A book's connection to Audiobookshelf is tied to the server it came from. If you reconnect to a different server, re-downloading books from the original one won't work until you're connected to that one again.
</Callout>
