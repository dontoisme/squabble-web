Squabble's web app already shows your library and guild activity, but it doesn't play audio yet — for any book, from any source. Streaming an [Audiobookshelf](/help/audiobookshelf/what-is-audiobookshelf) book straight from your browser is a coming-soon feature, not something you can do today.

## What works today

Your library list, progress, notes, and guild activity are all visible on the web <PlatformBadge platform="web" />, including books you've downloaded from your Audiobookshelf server on mobile — you can see them and track where you are in each one. What the web app doesn't do yet is play anything back. Connecting a server, browsing its library, downloading a book, and listening are all mobile-only today — see [Browsing and downloading](/help/audiobookshelf/browsing-and-downloading) for how that works on your phone.

## What's coming

<PlatformBadge platform="coming-soon" /> In-browser streaming will let you connect your Audiobookshelf server from the web and play a book directly from it — no download to a device required, and no separate setup beyond the server connection itself. Seeking, chapters, and your listening position are planned to work the same way they do on mobile, with progress staying in sync with both your server and your guild.

<Callout variant="info">
This is planned work, not a current feature. There's no setting to turn on and nothing to try yet — check back here once it ships.
</Callout>
