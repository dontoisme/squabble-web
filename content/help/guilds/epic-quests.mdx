An **Epic Quest** is a whole book series tracked as a single quest on your [guild's](/help/guilds/guilds-overview) board. Instead of adding five separate books, you add the saga once and Squabble follows each Adventurer through it, book by book.

## What an Epic Quest tracks

An Epic Quest bundles an ordered series — say, all of *The Kingkiller Chronicle* — into one entry. It keeps:

- The **ordered list of books** in the series.
- Each Adventurer's position: which book they're currently on and which ones they've finished.
- The guild's overall place in the series.

On the [Quest Board](/help/guilds/quest-board), an Epic Quest folds its books into a collapsible group rather than scattering them across the board, so a long series stays tidy.

## The community discovery index

Series data in Squabble is **community-contributed**. There's no central catalog maintained by a publisher — the first guild to group a set of books into a series discovers it and forges the Epic Quest. From then on, that series lives in a shared discovery index, so the next guild to pick up the same books finds the Epic Quest already mapped out.

<Callout variant="info">
Because the index is shared across Squabble, the more guilds that read, the richer the map of series becomes. When you forge a new Epic Quest, you're leaving a trail for every guild that comes after you.
</Callout>

## Guild Titles

Epic Quests are how your guild — not just individual Adventurers — earns honor. As a guild discovers and completes Epic Quests, it earns guild **Titles**:

- **Cartographer** — for mapping new territory by discovering Epic Quests.
- **Lorekeeper** — for the lore your guild banks by completing Epic Quests.
- **Trailblazer** — for completing an Epic Quest your own guild discovered.
- **Chronicler** — for the guild's broader record of finished sagas.

These guild Titles are distinct from the personal Titles an individual Adventurer equips on their profile. A guild's earned Titles appear alongside its name, a record of the parties it has seen through to the end.

<Callout variant="spoiler">
Discussion inside an Epic Quest is still spoiler-safe. Comments and Topics stay tied to a moment in a specific book and only unlock for a guildmate once they reach that point — so being three books ahead in the series never spoils book one for someone just starting out.
</Callout>

## Reading an Epic Quest

There's no separate mode to enter. You read each book in the series the same way you read any Quest — in the player, leaving [timestamp comments](/help/player/timestamp-comments) and starting [Topics](/help/guilds/tavern-chat-and-topics). The Epic Quest simply stitches those books together and keeps score of where everyone is across the whole series.
