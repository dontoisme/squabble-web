A **Solo Quest** lets you share your reading of a book that *isn't* on the [Quest Board](/help/guilds/quest-board) — a side quest you picked up on your own — with guildmates who happen to own the same book. It's how a private read becomes a soft "you should read this" without making it an official guild Quest.

## What sharing does

By default, a book that's only in your personal library is invisible to your guild. Turn on its Solo Quest and two things change:

- Your guild's [Activity feed](/help/guilds/activity-feed-and-reactions) shows milestones — you "embarked on a side quest" when you start and "completed a side quest" when you finish.
- Your [timestamp comments](/help/player/timestamp-comments) on that book are saved to the guild, where guildmates who also own the book can discover them.

If you leave a Solo Quest off, the book stays completely private and your comments are just personal notes.

## Turning on a Solo Quest

The toggle lives on the book's detail screen, below its guild status.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the book's details">
    From your library, tap the book to open its detail screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Solo Quest section">
    Scroll to the <strong>Solo Quest</strong> section. It appears only when you're in a guild and the book isn't already on the Quest Board.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share with guild">
    Turn on <strong>Share with guild</strong>. Sharing is off by default and set per book, so nothing goes out until you opt in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The two-gate visibility rule

Comments on a Solo Quest book are protected by two gates. A guildmate has to clear **both** before they can see one of your comments:

- **Gate 1 — Progress.** Have they listened past the moment your comment is pinned to? This is the same spoiler gate that protects every timestamp comment.
- **Gate 2 — Ownership.** Do they actually own this book in their own library?

<Callout variant="spoiler">
Both gates apply at once. A guildmate who owns the book but hasn't reached your comment still won't see it, and a guildmate who's finished the book but doesn't own it won't either. Only someone who owns the book *and* has listened past that point gets to read what you wrote.
</Callout>

## Turning sharing back off

You can stop sharing a Solo Quest at any time, and it's honest, reversible behavior:

- Comments you already shared stay visible to guildmates who own the book.
- New comments you write go back to being private notes.

Nothing is deleted, and you can turn sharing back on later to resume.

## Promoting to a full Quest

A Solo Quest is a gentle recommendation. When you finish one and a guildmate sees the milestone in the feed, they get an **Add to Quest Board** button — one tap turns your side quest into a shared guild Quest that everyone can take on. That's the whole recommendation mechanism: no separate "recommend" button, just the trail you left behind.
