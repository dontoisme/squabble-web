Once your shelf has more than a handful of books, Squabble gives you a few ways to slice it: filter chips, a sort menu, a card/list toggle, and folder browsing. Your library is also viewable on the web <PlatformBadge platform="web" />.

## Filtering your shelf

A row of chips sits under the Library header: **All**, **In Progress**, **Completed**, **Not Started**, **In Guild**, **Not on Device**, and **Archived**. The row scrolls horizontally, so swipe left to reach the later chips.

<Callout variant="info" title="Some chips come and go">
<strong>In Guild</strong>, <strong>Not on Device</strong>, and <strong>Archived</strong> only appear once you have a book in that state. If you unarchive your last archived book, for example, the Archived chip disappears and the filter snaps back to All.
</Callout>

## Card view vs. list view

Books display as a grid of cover-art **cards** by default. Switch to compact **list** rows from the sort menu (tap the funnel icon near the top of the Library) if you'd rather scan titles quickly. Selecting multiple books for bulk actions always switches you into list rows automatically.

## Sorting your books

From that same sort menu, choose how the All Books view is ordered:

- **Recently Played** (default)
- **Date Added**
- **Title A-Z**
- **Author A-Z**
- **Progress**

<Screenshot src="/help/library/library_04_sort_menu.png" alt="The view and sort menu, showing By Author, By Series, and By Narrator grouping options plus sort-by choices" caption="Switch between grouping and sorting from the same menu." />

## Browsing by folder

The same menu's **View** section lets you group your whole library instead of sorting it flat: **By Author**, **By Series**, or **By Narrator**. Each group renders as a folder tile — tap one to open its contents. Series folders sort by book position by default so a series reads in order; author and narrator folders sort alphabetically by title.

<Callout variant="tip">
Folders only include books with audio on your device — a book that's missing its file shows up in the <strong>Not on Device</strong> section instead, not inside its author or series folder.
</Callout>

Tapping any book from a card, list row, or folder opens its [book details](/help/library/book-details) screen, where playback, comments, and guild actions live.
