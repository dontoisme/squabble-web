Sometimes a book is in your library but its audio file isn't on your phone — after a reinstall, a manual file deletion, or mid-download. This page covers what you'll see and how to recover it.

## The Not on Device section

Any book whose audio isn't on your device collects under a collapsible **Not on Device** section (and its own filter chip) instead of mixing in with your playable books. Each tile tells you why:

- **Audio missing** (folder icon) — a locally imported book whose file is gone. Tap it to re-import.
- **Re-download from ABS** (cloud icon) — a book that came from an [Audiobookshelf](/help/audiobookshelf/connecting-your-server) server. Tap it to pull the audio back down.

## Download progress

While a book's audio is downloading, its card shows a live "Downloading N%" label with a thin progress bar underneath. Tapping a book that's still downloading just lets you know it's not ready yet — there's nothing to play until it finishes.

## Recovering a single missing book

Tapping a missing book prompts you to either bring it back or set it aside:

- **Re-download** / **Re-import** — for an Audiobookshelf book, this reconnects and re-downloads automatically (prompting you to sign back in first if your session expired); for a locally imported book, it reopens your file picker so you can choose the file again.
- **Move to Archive** — if you don't want the book back right now. See [managing your books](/help/library/managing-books) for what archiving does.

Either path keeps your progress and comments exactly where they were.

## After a reinstall

If your whole library shows as missing at once — the classic post-reinstall moment — Squabble swaps the Library screen for a dedicated **Restore your books** list instead of scattering everything under one filter. Tap a book to bring it back individually, or use **Select books to archive** to bulk-clear anything you don't want to restore.

<Callout variant="tip" title="Skip the scramble next time">
Set up a [library storage folder](/help/library/importing-books) the first time you import a book. With a folder in place, Squabble can often find your files again after a reinstall instead of needing everything re-downloaded or re-imported from scratch.
</Callout>
