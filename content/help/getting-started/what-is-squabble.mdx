Squabble Inn is a social audiobook player. You bring your own audiobooks, listen in a beautifully crafted player, and share the journey with a **Guild** — a private group of up to 6 readers. <PlatformBadge platform="mobile" />

<Screenshot src="/help/getting-started/ftue_00_splash.png" alt="The Squabble Inn signboard splash screen, a wooden sign hanging from a tree branch reading Squabble Inn, Est. 2026" caption="Every Adventurer's journey starts at the same front door." />

## Read together, spoil nothing

The heart of Squabble is spoiler-safe discussion. Leave a [timestamp comment](/help/player/timestamp-comments) at any moment in a book, and it stays hidden from your guildmates until they reach that point themselves.

<Callout variant="spoiler">
Comments are locked to listening progress. A guildmate three chapters behind you will never see your reaction to the big twist — until they've heard it too.
</Callout>

## The three halls of the Inn

- **Library** — import your audiobooks, organize them, and track progress. Your library also appears on the web. <PlatformBadge platform="web" />
- **Player** — playback with chapters, bookmarks, speed control, a Sleep Timer, and live **Ghost Markers** showing where your guildmates are.
- **Guild** — the social hall: the [Quest Board](/help/guilds/quest-board), activity feed, Adventurers roster, and the Tavern chat.

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Sign up with email, Google, or Apple. See [Creating your account](/help/getting-started/creating-your-account).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a book">
    Import an audiobook file or connect an [Audiobookshelf server](/help/audiobookshelf/connecting-your-server).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join or found a Guild">
    Enter a 6-character invite code from a friend, or create your own Guild and invite others.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The first time you sign in, Squabble walks you through these three steps in order — see the [onboarding tour](/help/getting-started/onboarding-tour) for a screen-by-screen look, or the [quick start](/help/getting-started/quick-start) if you'd rather jump straight in.
